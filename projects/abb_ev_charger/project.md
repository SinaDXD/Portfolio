## Short Description
Developing a new family of EV chargers for electric cars, comprising three models: a slow charger, a fast American charger, and a fast European charger. The chargers share a cohesive design language reflecting ABB’s company identity and brand essence. Teammate: Kosar Leyli Kazemi.

## Overview
A collaborative project between FHNW Masterstudio Industrial Design and the ABB Research Center in Baden. Together with a colleague, I was selected by FHNW to lead the design development of a new family of electric vehicle (EV) chargers. The goal was to translate ABB’s engineering excellence into a cohesive, user-centered product line.

## Challenge
Design a unified family of three EV chargers—comprising a slow charger, a fast American charger, and a fast European charger—that reflects ABB’s brand identity. The products needed to deliver an intuitive user experience while aligning with technical, ergonomic, and sustainability requirements across diverse global markets.

## Solution
We began with a collaborative kickoff and in-depth research into ABB’s brand values, user journeys, and pain points. We focused on enhancing usability through subtle yet effective interaction design, most notably, a built-in status light that clearly communicates charging progress at a glance. We also integrated circular design strategies to support longevity: modular construction, easy maintenance, and reduced material complexity were core principles of our approach.

## Result
The final design is a robust, visually cohesive product family that elevates the everyday charging experience. It embodies ABB’s technological leadership while addressing the practical needs of diverse users and stakeholders. Our solution supports both brand continuity and long-term sustainability goals through thoughtful interaction, material efficiency, and maintainability.
